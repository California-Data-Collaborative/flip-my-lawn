How Flip My Lawn works

Define your project

Search for your home to view estimates of the total square footage of lawn available for conversion using aerial imagery.

You can fine tune the estimates by outlining the specific areas you are considering converting.

View potential savings

Using lawn area estimates and measurments of local environmental conditions, we calculate the potential water, carbon, and dollar savings.

Flip your lawn

After viewing your potential savings, you can search for rebate opportunities, native plants, local nurseries, and vetted landscape contractors.

Here in California, we can save water, energy, (and some money too) by converting our lawns to beautiful California native landscapes. Flip My Lawn was built to help streamline this conversion process. Here you will find estimates of potential savings and customized resources for next steps. Please remember that the savings quoted are only estimates and may be optimistic. Current imagery is from 2016. For more details about our approach, please take a look at our methodology.

Flip My Lawn is a joint effort of the California Data Collaborative and ARGO, and it is funded by an Innovative Conservation Program grant.

Your next steps

Save Money

Most water districts offer rebates for turf removal and/or efficient irrigation equipment. Check your local utility's website.

Find rebates

Choose Plants

Not all CA native plants are native to all of CA. Find plants native to your area for your garden.

Locate Nurseries

Not all nurseries stock native plants. Find local nurseries that do. Even if you don't plan on "Doing It Yourself," being aware local nurseries that stock natives can help inform conversations with a landscape contractor.

Find nurseries

Contact Qualified Contractors

Now that you have done some research, if you don't plan on landscaping yourself, the final step in flipping your lawn is to reach out to a landscape contractor. Below you can find links to help you find local landscapers that have been certified to install and maintain water-efficient landscapes.

CLCA Certified

CLCA certified landscape professionals must pass a written test, complete an irrigation audit, and demonstrate proficiency on landscape jobs for one year before becoming fully certified.

Find a CLCA certified landscaper
QWEL Certified

QWEL certified landscape professionals must pass a written test, complete an irrigation audit, and complete 20 hours of classroom learning covering irrigation systems, landscape design and water budgeting before becoming fully certified.
