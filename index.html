How Flip My Lawn works

1. Define your project

Search for your home to view estimates of the total square footage of lawn available for conversion using aerial imagery.

You can fine tune the estimates by outlining the specific areas you are considering converting.

2. View potential benefits

Using lawn area estimates and measurments of local environmental conditions, we calculate the potential water, carbon, and dollar savings.

Converting your lawn to natives benefits both you and the environment.

3. Flip your lawn

After viewing the potential benefits, you can search for rebate opportunities, native plants, local nurseries, and vetted landscape contractors.

These resources will help you save money and flip your lawn with confidence.

Here in California, we can save water, energy, (and some money too) by converting our lawns to beautiful California native landscapes. Flip My Lawn was built to help streamline this conversion process. Here you will find estimates of potential benefits and customized resources for next steps. Please remember that the numbers quoted are only estimates and may be optimistic. Default turf estimates are from 2016. For more details about our approach, please take a look at our methodology.

Flip My Lawn is a joint effort of the California Data Collaborative and ARGO, and it is funded by an Innovative Conservation Program grant.

Contact Qualified Contractors

Now that you have done some research, if you don't plan on landscaping yourself, the final step in flipping your lawn is to reach out to a landscape contractor. Below you can find links to help you find local landscapers that have been certified to install and maintain water-efficient landscapes.

California Landscape Contractors Association (CLCA)

CLCA is the largest landscape contractors association in California. They offer multiple certification programs to members focused on efficiently managing water as well as installing and maintaining native landscapes. Certified contractors have passed a written test, an irrigation audit and actively managed landscapes at or below specified water budgets. Follow the link below to find landscapers near you with certified employees on staff.

Find a CLCA certified landscaper
Qualified Water Efficient Landscaper (QWEL)

QWEL is an EPA endorsed certification program in irrigation system audits. Landscape professionals complete 20 hours of education on local water supply, sustainable landscaping, soils, landscape water budgets, irrigation system components and maintenance, irrigation system audits, and scheduling and controller programming. They must then pass a written exam and demonstrate their ability to perform an irrigation system audit.
